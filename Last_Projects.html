MIT Project

Computer Science Principles

October 2019

The objective of our MIT project was to build an MIT app based on what we had learned. This was another project where teamwork was necessary, as we were tasked with working with a partner. My partner and I created a fun Mini Golf Game. What I learned from this project is that by setting my mind to doing something, there is a lot I can do. As an example, we had initially wanted to put a lake obstacle in our game, but we had agreed that we would probably have no time for that. To our surprise, we were able to add the lake, along with other features. We overcame the challenge of our close-mindedness, and were able to improve our game. Again, my partner and I worked well together, becuase we were able to communicate and coordinate the order of events very well. In this project, I was struggling less for time, and instead was working more towards using extra time to improve our game.











Scratch project

Computer Science Principles

September 2019

The objective of the project was to build a fully functional, fun game that would demonstrate our knowledge of Scratch programming, and highlight our abilities to work with a partner. My role in the game development rotated between 'navigator' and 'driver'. My partner and I would either be the one leading by giving instructions, while the other one would be the one executing the instructions. I learned that it's better to set a timeline of events to accomplish, rather than working randomly and out of order. This way, we can accomplish our goals by a set date instead of prolonging them until the deadline approaches. Challenges we overcame were slight glitches in the game, including glitches where the dog would not jump. We also learnt to spent less time on the actual appearance of the game, and more on the internal coding. My partner and I worked well together becuase we were able to utilize the strengths of each other to make our project even more fun and interesting to play. Find our Project!
